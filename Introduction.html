Skin Cancer Classifier

Our skin cancer classifier leverages the power of Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN) to accurately identify various skin cancer types from medical images. With the capability of deep learning algorithms to analyze complex patterns in images, our system efficiently classifies skin lesions into distinct categories, aiding in early detection and diagnosis.

The classifier can recognize the following skin cancer types:

  • Actinic keratoses
  • Basal cell carcinoma
  • Benign keratosis-like lesions
  • Dermatofibroma
  • Melanocytic nevi
  • Melanoma
  • Vascular lesions

Melanocytic nevi

Melanocytic nevi, or moles, are benign skin growths formed by clusters of melanocytes, the pigment-producing cells. They can vary in size, color, and shape, often appearing as brown or black spots. Moles can be congenital (present at birth) or acquired due to genetic factors or sun exposure.
Rarity : Very common.
Treatability : Harmless; removable if needed.

Melanoma

Melanoma is a severe, aggressive form of skin cancer originating in melanocytes. It presents as asymmetrical, irregularly colored lesions and can metastasize, requiring early detection and treatment.
Rarity : Rare but serious.
Treatability : Treatable if detected early.

Benign keratosis-like lesions

These non-cancerous lesions include seborrheic keratoses and solar lentigines. Common in older adults, they appear as rough, pigmented patches due to aging or sun damage.
Rarity : Very common.
Treatability : Harmless; removable for cosmetic or irritation.

Basal cell carcinoma

BCC is the most common skin cancer, arising from basal cells. Slow-growing and rarely metastatic, it often manifests as shiny nodules or ulcers in sun-exposed areas.
Rarity : Very common.
Treatability : Highly treatable with early intervention.

Dermatofibromas

Dermatofibromas are benign, firm, skin-colored nodules commonly found on the legs. Often asymptomatic, they may itch or hurt upon pressure but rarely require treatment.
Rarity : Relatively uncommon.
Treatability : Harmless; removable if desired.

Actinic keratoses

Actinic keratoses are rough, scaly patches caused by prolonged UV exposure. Considered precancerous, they can progress to squamous cell carcinoma if untreated, necessitating timely intervention.
Rarity : Common in sun-exposed individuals.
Treatability : Treatable with cryotherapy or topical treatments.
